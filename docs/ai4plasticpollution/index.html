In 1907, Belgian chemist, Leo Baekeland, patented the first fully synthetic plastic. He named it Bakelite. Then, by accident, polyethylene was discovered again in the 1930s (after the accidental invention in 1898). Its first use was insulating radar cabling during the 2nd world war. But soon, it was used in consumer products like Tupperware, shopping bags, etc. But sadly, the properties of plastic which make it so useful, also make it a curse to our environment. A plastic bottle can last for more than 4 centuries in oceans. Then too, it only breaks down to microscopic particles and never really goes away.

Then came polyethylene terephthalate (PET) in 1973. It along with polyethylene bags and other plastics increased the usage of single-use plastics by many folds. And our plastic problems got exponentially worse. Today, plastic pollution is one of the most critical environmental hazards. I think, most people will agree with me. Writing just about the intensity of plastic pollution can be a blog in itself. Today, plastics can be found in the Antarctic ice and our bodies too!!
